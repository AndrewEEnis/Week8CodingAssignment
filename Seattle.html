Seattle

The City Itself

Seattle is a seaport city on the West Coast of the United States. It is the seat of King County, Washington. With a 2020 population of 737,015, it is the largest city in both the state of Washington and the Pacific Northwest region of North America. The Seattle metropolitan area's population is 4.02 million, making it the 15th-largest in the United States. Its growth rate of 21.1% between 2010 and 2020 made it one of country's fastest-growing large cities.

Interesting Facts About Seattle

  1. The Space Needle opened for the Seattle World's fair in 1962. The original design was sketched on a napkin.
  2. Seattle’s annual rainfall is less than that of Houston, Chicago and New York City.
  3. Seattle is home to the world’s first floating bridge AND the world’s longest and widest floating bridge.
  4. The University of Washington is one of three schools in the U.S. where you can arrive at the football stadium by boat.
  5. People in Seattle buy the most sunglasses per capita than any other U.S. city.
  6. Seattle has nearly 500 houseboats, more than anywhere else in the world.
  7. The world’s largest floating home is located on Lake Union in Seattle
  8. The University Bridge gets regular baths to protect it from the heat during the summer.
  9. The Washington State Ferry System is the largest in the country and the third largest in the world, carrying more than 25 million passengers each year.
  10. At 76 stories and 937 feet, the black-colored Columbia Center is Seattle’s tallest building and is the 12th tallest building in the country.
